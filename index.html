Data Science for Climate and Extremes

Welcome! My name is Dr. Maria J. Molina (she/her) and I am thrilled to share that my research group is launching this upcoming Fall 2022! Our research will focus on the application of machine learning tools (e.g. neural networks) and numerical modeling systems (e.g. CESM) to answer pressing questions in the domains of climate and extremes. Examples of problems that we will be tackling include the following:

☁ extending Earth system prediction,
☁ understanding genesis of extremes, and
☁ uncovering multi-scale patterns in the climate system.

Our research will also strive to incorporate open-source software and data, accessible communication, multi-discipline collaboration, and stakeholder engagement.

Teaching

In addition to research check-ins and discussions, our research group will set time aside for soft-skills training. Studies have shown that career success can be attributed to soft-skills by as much as 75%. Biweekly meetings will be held to help address this critically important training, along with other needed skills for graduate school.
